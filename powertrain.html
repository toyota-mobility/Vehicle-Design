Engines for Any Kind of Drive

Internal Combustion Engines

This powertrain class relies on the combustion of a volatile fuel (i.e. gasoline) and an oxidizer (i.e. air) to generate a mechanical force.

Toyota's latest innovation in this space is the twin-turbo V6 engine. The engine uses turbine power to push extra compressed air into the combustion chamber resulting in a boost of horsepower and torque.

Hybrid Electric Motors

Hybrid systems incorporate both a traditional gas-powered engine and a battery powered electric motor. The motor assists the gas-engine during acceleration to high speeds and cruising at low speeds for savings on fuel consumption.

The electric motors used for Plug-in Hybrids have been advanced to provide direct power instead of serving solely as a generator. With a larger lithium-ion battery, a crusing range of 60 mph can be acheived in purely EV mode.

Hydrogen Fuel Cells

As an alternative to battery derived electricity, power is generated from fuel cells that draw hydrogen from a pressurized tank in the vehicle. The hydrogen drawn reacts chemically with oxygen to produce the electricity required for the motor. PEMs (Polymer Electroly Membranes) seperate a series of thin plates that make up the fuel cells and is where the chemical reaction takes place.

With only water as a byproduct, this powertrain allows for zero carbon emission driving.
