Text-to-Rendering Design

Leveraging generative AI, Toyota engineers can produce a slew of aerodynamic vehicle designs in an instant.

Words such as "sleek" and "modern" are submitted as parameters and balanced with aerodynamic principles to achieve viable models that otherwise might never have come to light.

Features & Shapes

To acheive the award winning fuel-savings Toyota products are known for, engineers apply alterations to the body design for reducing unwanted airstream interference.

For instance, a small upper grill aperture can be added to reduce the initial impact of air directing it across the body of the car instead of through it. A chin spoiler at the bottom splices the air so that it does not pass underneath the car. Finally, spats can be attached to the front of each wheel to diminish airflow around the tires.

Inspiration From Airplane Simulations

Leveraging simulation models relied on for airplane testing, Toyota Motor Corp. engineers learned how to create a vortex around the Lexus LC-500 rendering higher performance of the vehicle.

High performance computing allowed for processing & visualization of simulation data which then served as inputs to the new Lexus design.
