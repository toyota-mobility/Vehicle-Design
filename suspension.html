X-REAS on 4-Runner TRD Sport

Cross Linked Relative Absorber System uses a series of adaptive dampers to adjust the chassis for on-roading performance.

The vehicle's shocks are cross-diagonally paired via hydraulic chambers that divert force to the shock experiencing the greater force of the two. This offsetting allows the wheels to remain in contact with the road and lessen sway, pitch & roll for a smoother ride.

Track-tuned Racing Suspension on Camry-TRD

With stabilizer bars, shock absorbers and rear coil springs, this suspension system allows the Camry-TRD to sit lower to the ground. Increased torisional rigidity allows for the tight turns required for racing on modern tracks.

KDSS on Toyota Land Cruiser

Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System features anti-roll bars with hydraulic cylinders. The stabilizer bars are decoupled when differences in hydraulic pressure are detected from imbalances in wheel contact with the ground, typical of offroad settings. The cylinders also supress suspension stroke on sharp turns for greater stability.
